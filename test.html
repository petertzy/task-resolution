How to Reduce Apple App Store and Google Play Store Fees

If you don’t distribute digital subscriptions through the Apple App Store or Google Play Store, you don’t need to pay any transaction fees. However, the claim that “all apps must pay a 30% commission” is not entirely accurate. Both Apple and Google have tiered fee structures, allowing some developers to qualify for reduced fees.

Latest Apple & Google App Store Fee Policies (as of 2024)

Apple App Store (iOS):

  • Standard Fee: 30% (applies to most app subscriptions and in-app purchases).
  • Reduced Fee: 15%
    • Available to developers with annual revenue below $1 million.
    • Applies to subscriptions after the first year (first year remains 30%).

Google Play Store (Android):

  • Standard Fee: 30% (for in-app purchases and subscriptions).
  • Reduced Fee: 15% (applies to all subscription services from the first year).
  • Alternative Payment Systems: Some regions allow third-party payments, lowering fees to 11-26%.

Strategies to Reduce App Store Fees

1. Apply for Official Discount Programs (Reduce to 15%)

  • Apple App Store: App Store Small Business Program
    • Developers earning less than $1 million annually can apply.
    • Apple’s commission is reduced to 15% instead of 30%.
    • Official Application Link
  • Google Play Store: All subscription-based apps automatically qualify for the 15% fee from the first year.

Who can benefit?

  • Small developers earning under $1 million per year.
  • Apps offering subscription services.

2. Use External Payment Methods to Avoid High Fees

Apple and Google allow developers to redirect users to their website for payments, avoiding high commissions.

  • Apple: External Link Account Entitlement
    • Used by companies like Spotify and Netflix to direct users to subscribe on their websites.
  • Google: User Choice Billing
    • In certain countries, users can choose between Google Play or third-party payment providers.

How to implement this?

  1. Remove in-app subscriptions and replace them with a “Subscribe on Website” button.
  2. Offer Stripe, PayPal, or credit card payments on your website to avoid in-app purchase fees.

Key Considerations:

  • Apple still charges 27% for external payments (slightly lower than 30%).
  • Google’s third-party payment fees vary between 11-26% depending on the region.

3. Use a Web App (PWA) to Bypass App Stores

For subscription-based or SaaS apps (like Notion, Spotify, or Netflix), you can build a PWA (Progressive Web App) instead of distributing through app stores.

Advantages:

  • No commissions from Apple or Google; all transactions happen directly on your website.
  • Ideal for SaaS businesses that don’t require app store distribution.

Examples:

  • YouTube Premium: No in-app subscription on iOS; users must subscribe on the website.
  • Spotify & Netflix: Direct users to their websites for payments instead of in-app purchases.

Implementation Steps:

  1. Develop a fully functional PWA so users can access your service via a web browser.
  2. Set up website payments and guide users to subscribe through their browser.
  3. Use email, SMS, or notifications to remind users about renewals.

4. Optimize Payment Options for Different Regions

Some countries have regulations that allow developers to bypass or reduce App Store fees.

  • European Union (EU): Due to antitrust laws, Google allows third-party payments, reducing fees to 11%.
  • South Korea, India, Japan: These governments require Google to support alternative payment methods.

Recommended Actions:

  • For EU markets: Use Google User Choice Billing to lower fees.
  • For South Korea & India: Integrate local payment systems (e.g., KakaoPay in South Korea, Paytm in India) to avoid in-app purchase fees.

5. Avoid Apple/Google Subscription APIs When Possible

Apple and Google require developers to use their official subscription APIs (In-App Purchase, IAP), which means:

  • You cannot provide external payment links within the app.
  • All in-app digital purchases (such as subscriptions and virtual goods) are subject to 30% or 15% commissions.

Possible Solutions:

  1. For SaaS apps (e.g., enterprise tools): Provide only a web version to avoid in-app transactions.
  2. For subscription-based models: Offer long-term plans to benefit from Apple's reduced 15% fee after one year.

Summary: How to Minimize Apple & Google Fees

If you want to completely avoid App Store fees:

  • Use a Web App or PWA and direct users to subscribe on your website.
  • Encourage users to pay via your website instead of in-app purchases.

If you want to reduce fees while remaining in app stores:

  • Apply for the 15% Small Business Program (for eligible developers).
  • Use third-party payment options in supported countries to lower commissions.

Final Thoughts

  • PWA/Web Apps are the most cost-effective solution but have limitations.
  • Guiding users to pay on your website can significantly reduce App Store fees.
  • Regional regulations vary, so take advantage of local policies to lower costs.

If your goal is to reduce App Store/Play Store fees and provide users with easier access, then PWA + external payments is the best strategy.
